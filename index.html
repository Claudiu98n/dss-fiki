Problem

Shopping in a group can be a difficult thing because you have to split the shopping list to all the people equally taking into account the budget allocated to each person and that can be a real challenge sometimes. Also, some product's prices change often and if we want to save some money we should keep track of price changes over time in various stores and this requires a lot of time and energy.

Customer segment

Given the fact that our app tries to combat the old-fashioned way of tracking down the items that need to be bought using pen and paper, the customer segment that we are targeting is the young man/ woman with ages ranging from 18 years old to 35 years old.

Advantage over competition

All the main competitors of our app lack the option of calculating the cost that users have to pay in order to purchase their share of products when using the group shopping feature. Furthermore, all the apps listed above lack a friendly user interface which clearly makes the young audience keep scrolling.

Cost structure

  • Hardware maintenance
  • Marketing
  • Services
  • QA

Our Solution

Our solution consists in a mobile application that is able to manage group shopping, keep track of product's prices over time and calculate the user's total spendings. Users can visualize in real time products purchased by other users in the group, group total spendings and user spendings for all the members in the group. Users can monitor the price evolution of a product over time, based on all the other users' purchases of the same product.

Competition

  • AnyList (Free, available for iOS and Android)
  • Bring! Grocery Shopping List (Free, available for iOS and Android )
  • Flipp (Free, available for iOS and Android)
  • Our Groceries (Free, available for iOS and Android)

Key metrics

  • Users downloading our app per month
  • Average time spent by users in the app
  • User retention rate

Revenue streams

  • Pro subscription plan
  • Sponsored partnerships with shops for promoting products
  • Ads based revenue

Milestone 5

To promote the application on Facebook, we created a page where we distributed our product's landing page. The promotion of the post and the page took place through Facebook ads and distribution in groups.

Permalink

The post had 24 engagements, it reached 375 people and had only 7 link clicks.

From the ads, people did not interact more, even if the ads reached 294 people, only one person accessed the link.

The post was distributed to different categories of groups to observe the interest of as many age groups as possible. We distributed it to student groups, advertising groups from the Bucharest area and startup groups.

Conclusions

Being at the beginning, the impact was quite small. From the posts on Facebook, only 8 people visited the page, but on the page the people seemed interested in the idea, wanting to know more about the application. Promoting on multiple Facebook and WhatsApp groups and continuing to use ads on Facebook can increase the number of interested people and user traffic on the landing page.
